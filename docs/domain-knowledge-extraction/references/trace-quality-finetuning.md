## LLM Trace Quality and Fine-Tuning Architecture

### Core Concepts

**The fluency-accuracy dissociation is the central threat to synthetic poker training data.** PokerBench demonstrated 28.5% accuracy from untrained LLMs producing fluent justifications — the model recalls "MDF" and "blocker effects" as co-occurring concepts in poker reasoning but cannot compute the directional implication of holding a specific blocker on a specific board. This is not a knowledge gap; it is a binding failure. The model has the concept representations but cannot ground them in the combinatorial state. Fine-tuning on traces that exhibit the same dissociation — naming correct concepts, arriving at incorrect conclusions — reinforces the binding failure rather than correcting it.

**Solver-computed decisions are the only viable ground truth labels.** Expert human annotations fail for two reasons: (1) humans cannot reliably distinguish 0.2bb EV differences between mixed-strategy actions, and (2) human experts systematically over-weight narrative coherence ("this line tells a story") over frequency-optimal play. Solver EV deltas between actions provide the objective label; the trace's job is to produce the reasoning chain that connects game state to that label. A trace where the reasoning arrives at the correct action via incorrect logic is more dangerous than a trace that arrives at the wrong action via correct logic — the first teaches the model to pattern-match conclusions without mechanistic grounding.

**Trace quality exists on a 4-tier spectrum with sharp operational boundaries:**

- **Tier 0 (Noise):** Action contradicts solver output, or reasoning references game state elements not present in the input context. Inclusion at any volume degrades model calibration.
- **Tier 1 (Competent):** Correct action, generic reasoning. "Board is wet so we should bet smaller" without naming which draws are present, which combos in hero's range benefit, or what frequency. This is where the current Module 4 `SYSTEM_PROMPT` lands — it requests structured output but does not declare a quality floor.
- **Tier 2 (Expert):** Correct action with mechanistic reasoning: names specific combos ("KhQh has the second nut flush draw and blocks villain's Kx top pair combinations"), quantifies frequencies ("bet 33% pot at ~65% frequency with this merged equity distribution"), and connects sizing to polarity ("PI of 0.72 supports a 66% pot sizing; range is polar enough that small bets surrender too much equity to villain's middling hands").
- **Tier 3 (Practitioner):** Expert tier plus cross-street coherence, blocker cascade awareness, and explicit uncertainty quantification ("equity assumption is approximated on the turn — river action recommendations carry wider confidence intervals").

**The minimum board texture coverage matrix for generalization has 24 cells, not 12.** The 12 texture profiles (wetness × flush texture × pairing × connectivity) must each be represented at two SPR regimes (< 6 and >= 6) because strategy changes regime at that boundary. The coverage failure mode is specific and predictable: "interesting" boards (wet, two-tone, unpaired, connected — e.g., Jh9h7c) produce longer, more detailed traces and are naturally over-represented in any generation workflow that lets the operator choose boards. Dry boards (Kc7d2s rainbow) where the correct strategy is "range bet 33% pot at 80%+ frequency" produce short, repetitive traces that operators skip. The result: a fine-tuned model that over-bets dry boards because its training distribution associates board analysis with complex action recommendations.

**Minimum coverage thresholds per texture cell:** 15-25 traces per cell for a 24-cell matrix = 360-600 traces minimum before the model can generalize across board space. Below this, the model memorizes texture-action associations rather than learning the underlying equity distribution logic. The batch generation feature (P1 in CLAUDE.md) must iterate across a board matrix, not just the operator's current board.

**The `ev_tree_line` field is the strongest consistency anchor available in the current architecture.** Two traces generated from identical `ev_tree_line` inputs should produce frequency recommendations within 15pp of each other (consistent with the ±10-20pp uncertainty of GTO seeds documented in solver-theory-gto.md). Divergence beyond this threshold indicates the trace generator is hallucinating frequencies rather than deriving them from the EV tree. This is detectable without a solver: compare `frequency_recommendation_pct` in the trace output against the `villainFoldPct`/`villainCallPct` from the corresponding EV tree node.

**System prompt engineering for depth requires a floor declaration, not a ceiling aspiration.** The current `SYSTEM_PROMPT` says "produce a structured reasoning trace" and lists 7 requirements — this is a ceiling. It tells the model what a good trace looks like but does not tell it what to refuse to produce. The operational fix:

1. **Declare the floor explicitly:** "Do NOT explain what board texture is. Do NOT define range advantage. The consumer of this trace already knows GTO theory. Your job is to apply it to this specific game state."
2. **Require specific combo identification:** Add to the schema: `"key_combos": [{"hand": "AhKh", "role": "value/bluff/marginal", "frequency_pct": N, "reasoning": ""}]` — minimum 3 entries.
3. **Require frequency precision:** Change `"frequency_recommendation_pct": 0` to `"frequency_recommendation_pct": {"value": 0, "confidence": "high/medium/low"}` with the instruction that "high" means derived from EV tree or solver, "medium" means derived from heuristic (MDF, BE%), and "low" means estimated.
4. **Add rejection criteria to the prompt:** "If you cannot identify at least 3 specific combos driving the recommendation, output `{"quality_tier": "insufficient", "reason": "..."}` instead of a trace."

### Failure Modes and Anti-Patterns

**Severity 1: Concept-correct, conclusion-wrong traces at volume.** The highest-severity failure because it is invisible in spot-checking. A trace says "Hero holds Ah5h on Kh9h3c. The nut flush draw gives hero equity advantage and we should bet large for value." This names the correct draw, correctly identifies equity advantage, then recommends "betting for value" with a draw — conflating equity from draws (which need protection/semi-bluff framing) with equity from made hands. At volume, this teaches the model that draws = value, degrading its bet/check discrimination on flush-draw boards.

*Trigger:* Open-ended system prompt that does not require the model to classify hero's hand as value/bluff/marginal before recommending action.
*Detection:* Parse `action_evaluation[].rationale` for "value" co-occurring with draw hands identified in `classifyHand` as having `draws.length > 0` and `category` below "two pair."

**Severity 2: Fluent nonsense at training scale shifts calibration.** Including 500+ Tier 1 traces alongside 100 Tier 2 traces does not produce a model at Tier 1.5. The model's output distribution shifts toward the mode of the training data. 500 generic traces teach the model that generic reasoning is the expected output format, and the 100 expert traces become noise. This is the "more data is better" failure mode — it holds for classification tasks but inverts for generation tasks where output quality matters more than quantity.

*Trigger:* No quality gate between trace generation and JSONL inclusion. The current `generateTrace` function pushes every successful API response to `traceQueue` via `ADD_TRACE` regardless of content quality.
*Detection:* Compute trace length distribution. Tier 1 traces cluster at 300-500 tokens; Tier 2+ traces are 600-1200 tokens. A bimodal distribution with a large low-token cluster signals mixed-quality data.

**Severity 3: Cross-trace strategic inconsistency masquerading as mixing.** GTO strategies include mixed actions — the same hand bets 60% and checks 40%. But two traces for the same game state recommending different actions should both reference the mixing frequency. If trace A says "bet 66% pot, 100% frequency" and trace B says "check, 100% frequency" for the same board+range state, one of them is wrong. The model learns that both are valid, producing a fine-tuned model that randomly varies between pure strategies without understanding indifference.

*Trigger:* Batch generation across identical game states without `ev_tree_line` anchoring. When `ev_tree_line` is null (no Module 3 line selected), the trace generator has no frequency anchor and the LLM invents frequencies from its prior.
*Detection:* Group traces by `(hero_range, villain_range, board)` tuple. Within each group, compute variance of `frequency_recommendation_pct` for the same action. Variance > 400 (sd > 20pp) signals inconsistency, not mixing.

**Severity 4: Equity-action misalignment from static equity assumption.** The `equity_is_approximated` flag on turn/river EV tree nodes signals that equity has not been re-estimated for the narrowed ranges on later streets. A trace generated from a turn node uses flop equity (say 55%) but the actual turn equity after range narrowing might be 40% — the trace recommends a bet that is -EV given the true equity. The trace records the flop equity in `computed_metrics.hero_equity_pct`, and the reasoning built on that number is internally consistent but externally wrong.

*Trigger:* Generating traces from turn/river EV tree lines without re-running `runMonteCarlo` for the updated board.
*Detection:* Flag all traces where any node in `ev_tree_line` has `equity_is_approximated: true` — this matches the actual node-shape semantics from CLAUDE.md and solver-theory-gto.md rather than relying on `metadata.street`, which can falsely flag turn/river traces where the ev_tree_line begins at a flop node with reliable equity. These require manual review or automated equity re-check before inclusion.

**Severity 5: Board texture feature collision.** `boardTexture` returns a wetness score, but identical scores arise from strategically distinct boards (e.g., Jh9h7c and Qh8h3c both produce high wetness). If the system prompt only passes `wetness_score`, the LLM cannot distinguish between these boards and generates generic "wet board" reasoning. The trace sounds correct but is not board-specific.

*Trigger:* `buildContext()` passes only `wetness_score` from `metrics.wetness`, not the full `boardTexture` output (connectivity, flushTexture, isPaired).
*Detection:* Check `computed_metrics` in the serialized context — if only `wetness_score` is present without flush texture or connectivity detail, the trace lacks sufficient board state for Expert-tier reasoning.

### Implementation Notes

**`buildContext()` (Module 4, line ~1614)** serializes the game state for the API call. Currently missing from `computed_metrics`: `connectivity` (from `boardTexture`), `flushTexture` (rainbow/two-tone/monotone), `isPaired`, and the full `boardTexture` object. Adding these requires destructuring `metrics` to include the full `boardTexture` return value, which `Module2` already computes and stores.

**`SYSTEM_PROMPT` (Module 4, line ~1639)** is the single highest-leverage change for trace quality. The current prompt is 7 numbered requirements with a JSON schema. To push from Tier 1 to Tier 2:
- Add a floor declaration block before the requirements: `"The following are known to the consumer and must NOT be explained: [list floor concepts]"`
- Add `"key_combos"` array to the output schema requiring minimum 3 combo-specific entries
- Add `"quality_self_assessment"` field: `"tier_1_competent" | "tier_2_expert"` with the instruction that tier_1 outputs will be filtered
- Add `"frequency_source"` field: `"ev_tree" | "heuristic" | "estimated"` per action evaluation

**`generateTrace()` (Module 4, line ~1653)** needs a quality gate before `dispatch({ type: "ADD_TRACE" })`. Minimum gate criteria:
1. `trace.reasoning_trace.action_evaluation` has at least 2 entries with non-zero `ev_estimate_bb`
2. `trace.reasoning_trace.optimal_action.frequency_pct` is between 1 and 100 (not 0 or missing)
3. If `ev_tree_line` is present, `optimal_action.action` aligns with an action in the tree line (bet/check/fold matching)
4. `board_diagnosis` length > 100 characters (short diagnoses are Tier 0-1)

**`record.metadata` (Module 4, line ~1677)** should include: `board_texture_profile` (categorical: "wet_twotone_unpaired_connected"), `spr` (computed from `potSize`/`effStack`), `quality_tier` (from self-assessment or automated scoring), `ev_tree_anchored` (boolean: was `ev_tree_line` non-null). These enable post-hoc filtering and coverage analysis of the JSONL dataset.

**Batch generation (P1, not yet implemented)** must iterate across a board texture matrix, not just repeat the current board. The iteration should:
1. Define a board pool covering all 24 texture cells (wetness bucket × flush texture × pairing × SPR regime)
2. For each board, set `state.board` and re-run `runMonteCarlo` to get fresh equity
3. Generate trace with 500ms delay (already spec'd in CLAUDE.md)
4. Track coverage per texture cell in `batchProgress` state
5. Stop generating for over-represented cells (> 30 traces) and redirect to under-represented ones

**`traceQueue` (state field)** is the raw accumulator. A separate `curatedTraces` array (or filter function) should gate traces before JSONL export based on quality tier and consistency checks. The `exportJSONL` function should default to exporting only Tier 2+ traces, with an option to export all.

**`ev_tree_line` consistency check** requires comparing `frequency_recommendation_pct` from the trace against the corresponding node's `villainFoldPct`/`villainCallPct` in the EV tree. The EV tree node frequencies are GTO-seeded — if the trace's frequency recommendation diverges by more than 15pp from the GTO seed without explicit justification mentioning exploitative adjustment, the trace is internally inconsistent.

**`stampEV` and `computeNodeEV` (Module 3)** provide the EV anchor. After generating a trace, cross-reference `ev_estimate_bb` from the trace's `action_evaluation` against the `ev` field on the corresponding EV tree node. Divergence > 0.5bb signals the trace generated an EV estimate disconnected from the tree's recursive computation.

### "Looks Right But Isn't" Traps

**Trap 1: The polarity-sizing recommendation that ignores position.** A trace correctly identifies hero's range as polar (PI > 0.70) and recommends a large bet sizing. This looks expert. But if hero is OOP, equity realization dampens the effective polarity — OOP realizes 60-80% of raw equity, meaning the actual realized PI is lower than the computed PI. The trace should recommend a smaller sizing OOP or explicitly note the position-adjusted polarity. The current `buildContext` does not include position in `game_state`, so the LLM has no signal to make this adjustment. Every OOP trace that recommends large bets based on raw PI is subtly wrong.

*Why it's invisible:* The reasoning chain is internally valid given the inputs. The error is in the missing input (position), not the logic. Spot-checking the trace in isolation reveals nothing — you must cross-reference against the game state to notice position is absent.

**Trap 2: "EV-positive" traces that are calibrated to the wrong villain range.** The current pipeline uses the operator's selected villain range (stored in `state.villainRange`). If the operator selects a GTO range but generates traces framed as exploitative analysis, or selects a population range but generates traces framed as GTO-optimal, the trace's EV estimates are calibrated to a phantom opponent. The trace says "bet 66% pot for +1.2bb EV" — this is correct against the input range but the framing ("GTO-optimal") implies it would be correct against any competent opponent. A fine-tuned model learns to produce EV estimates without qualifying them against the assumed villain range.

*Why it's invisible:* The EV math in the trace is correct. The `metadata` records `range_advantage` and `nut_advantage` which are computed against the actual villain range. But neither the trace text nor the metadata records whether the villain range is GTO-derived or population-derived, making the trace's strategic framing unverifiable. Note that the overestimation is not symmetric — when exploitative fold frequencies are used without updating `equityAssumption` for the stronger calling range, the EV bias is directionally upward (exploitative EV is inflated), consistent with the exploitative-adjustments.md Trap 1 finding that `stampEV` recomputes EV after frequency edits but under the original equity assumption.

**Trap 3: Consistent-looking batches from identical system prompts.** A batch of 50 traces generated with the same `SYSTEM_PROMPT` will exhibit high surface consistency — similar sentence structures, similar concept ordering, similar frequency ranges. This looks like a well-calibrated dataset. In reality, it is a single model's output distribution being sampled 50 times. The consistency is stylistic, not strategic. Two traces for strategically different spots (dry board with nut advantage vs. wet board without it) that recommend different actions in the same prose style create a fine-tuning signal that the style is correct rather than that the strategic reasoning differs. Detection requires measuring strategic variance independently of stylistic variance — e.g., comparing the distribution of recommended actions across board textures rather than the textual similarity of reasoning chains.

*Why it's invisible:* Standard deduplication and consistency metrics (cosine similarity of embeddings, n-gram overlap) will show the batch as high-quality. The failure only surfaces when the fine-tuned model produces the same reasoning template for different spots, varying only the action conclusion.

**Trap 4: The `ev_estimate_bb` field that passes validation but encodes nonsense.** The trace schema requires `ev_estimate_bb` as a number. The LLM produces values like "+0.85" or "-0.32" that look reasonable. But without a solver, there is no ground truth for absolute EV. The LLM generates these numbers from its language prior — it has seen poker discussions with EV numbers and produces numbers in a plausible range. The numbers are not computed from the game state; they are hallucinated in-distribution. A quality gate checking "is `ev_estimate_bb` a reasonable number?" will pass. A quality gate checking "is `ev_estimate_bb` consistent with the equity and pot size?" can catch the worst cases: if hero has 55% equity in a 10bb pot, EV of betting should be roughly equity × pot minus investment, and values outside a 3bb window of this estimate are hallucinated.

*Why it's invisible:* The numbers look right. They are in the correct range. They even correlate weakly with equity (the LLM knows higher equity = higher EV). But the correlation is not causal — the model is not computing EV, it is predicting what EV numbers look like in poker analysis text.

### Connections to Adjacent Domains

**Solver Theory and GTO Equilibrium <-> Trace Quality:** Solver convergence artifacts (mixed frequencies that should be pure, unstable strategies from premature stopping) propagate directly into trace quality when solver outputs are used as labels. If a solver outputs 48% bet / 52% check for a hand that should be pure-bet (convergence artifact), the trace built on this label will justify mixing when no mix is strategically warranted. The trace generator cannot distinguish converged from unconverged solver output. The `ev_tree_line` GTO seeding formulas (`villainFoldPct = S / (P + 2S)`) are closed-form and convergence-artifact-free, but they are first-order approximations — traces anchored to these seeds are consistent but may diverge from full solver solutions by 5-15pp on specific runouts, particularly on boards where the abstraction choices (bet size bucketing) distort strategy. Traces should record `frequency_source: "gto_seed_formula"` vs `"solver"` vs `"estimated"` so consumers can weight them appropriately.

**Range Construction and Equity Distribution <-> Trace Quality:** The polarity index gap is the critical unresolved connection. `runMonteCarlo` returns mean equity, and the `histogram: null` field means the trace generator has no access to equity distribution shape. A trace that correctly identifies a polar range and recommends large sizing is doing so from the LLM's prior knowledge of poker, not from computed polarity data — the `polarity_index_hero` in `computed_metrics` is available but derived from `classifyHand` bucket ratios (an approximation), not from the actual equity histogram. When the polarity proxy disagrees with the true equity distribution (specifically on boards where Two Pair hands are misclassified as value on wet textures, per the Range Construction domain finding), the trace inherits the misclassification. Implementing `histogram` in `runMonteCarlo` and passing the distribution to the trace generator would allow computed polarity to replace heuristic polarity, upgrading every trace that references polarity from Tier 1 to Tier 2 grounding.

**Multi-Street Planning and EV Architecture <-> Trace Quality:** The `equity_is_approximated` flag on turn/river nodes is both a data quality signal and a trace generation constraint. Traces generated from nodes with this flag should include explicit uncertainty language ("equity assumption is static from flop; turn card may have shifted ranges significantly"). The `committed_fraction`, `street_equity_delta`, and `line_coherence` fields proposed in the Multi-Street Planning domain are not yet implemented in the EV tree node shape — when they are, they provide three additional consistency anchors for trace validation. A trace recommending "fold to raise" at SPR < 2 where `committed_fraction > 0.6` is strategically incoherent (already committed too much to fold), and this can be detected automatically.

**Bet Sizing Theory and Pot Geometry <-> Trace Quality:** The geometric sizing formula assumes villain calls all N streets, which breaks on static boards. A trace that recommends geometric sizing on Kc7d2s (where villain's calling range drops 40-60% between turn and river) looks expert but is miscalibrated. The `buildTree` bet sizings cap at 1.0x pot — traces cannot recommend overbets even when the game state warrants them (capped villain range + uncapped hero range). Any trace that recommends "large sizing" on a spot that requires an overbet is ceiling-constrained by the tree architecture, and this constraint should be recorded in metadata so the trace is not used to train the model against spots where overbets are correct.

**Exploitative Adjustments and Population Dynamics <-> Trace Quality:** The `villainProfile` concept from the Exploitative Adjustments domain creates a trace labeling problem. A trace generated against a population villain range (e.g., micro-stakes fold-to-cbet of 55%) encodes exploitative strategy. If this trace is labeled as "GTO analysis" in the training data, the fine-tuned model learns that exploitative frequencies are GTO. The current `record.metadata` includes `source: "RangeIQ-synthetic-v1"` but not `villain_range_type: "gto" | "population" | "custom"`. Without this label, a dataset mixing GTO and exploitative traces produces a model that outputs a blend of both without distinguishing when each is appropriate — the poker equivalent of mode collapse.
