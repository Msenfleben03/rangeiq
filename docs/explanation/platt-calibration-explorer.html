Platt Calibration Explorer

How probability calibration corrects model overconfidence and drives Kelly bet sizing

Model Probability vs Actual Win Rate

What is Platt calibration? Raw model probabilities are often overconfident -- a model that says "65% chance" might only win 58% of the time. Platt scaling fits a logistic regression on top of raw outputs to correct this systematic bias. The calibrated probabilities are then fed into the Kelly criterion for bet sizing.
P(win) = 1 / (1 + exp(-(6.4558 × model_prob - 3.8831)))

The Bug: Edge-Based vs Model-Prob-Based Calibration

What went wrong? The original implementation passed edge (model_prob - implied_prob) into the Platt calibrator instead of model_prob. Since edge values cluster near 0.05-0.25, the sigmoid mapped them to absurdly low calibrated probabilities (10-29%), regardless of whether the bet was a -410 heavy favorite or a +300 longshot. This made Kelly think every bet was a massive underdog, zeroing out all stakes.

Old Calibration BROKEN

Input: edge (0.03 to 0.23) → Output: P(win) = 11-29%

New Calibration FIXED

Input: model_prob (0.55 to 0.94) → Output: P(win) = 42-90%
