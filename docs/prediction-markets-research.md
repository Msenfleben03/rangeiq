## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Industry Overview

The prediction markets industry experienced explosive growth in 2025, reaching $40 billion in total transaction volume (400% YoY growth). The sector is projected to hit $1 trillion in annual trading volume by 2030, transitioning from niche speculation to mainstream financial infrastructure.

### Key Finding: STRUCTURAL INEFFICIENCY OPPORTUNITY

Political and economic prediction markets display significant inefficiencies that sophisticated quantitative models could exploit:

- **Arbitrage opportunities** existed on 95% of pre-election days (62 of 65 days)
- **Platform pricing divergence** creates cross-platform arbitrage
- **58% negative serial correlation** on Polymarket (price reversals = noise trading)
- **Institutional absence** creates dumb money dominance vs sports betting's sharp markets

### Strategic Recommendation: SELECTIVE INTEGRATION

**PROCEED** with focused entry into political/economic markets via Kalshi (US-regulated) and Polymarket (higher volume, crypto-based).
**AVOID** sports prediction markets - inferior pricing to traditional sportsbooks (4.77% vig vs 4.42% at FanDuel).
**PRIORITIZE** markets with academic evidence of inefficiency: elections, Fed decisions, inflation data, GDP forecasts.

## 4. TRADING MECHANICS

### Order Book vs AMM Models

#### **Order Book (Kalshi, Polymarket US, PredictIt)**

**Mechanism:** Traditional limit order book, maker-taker model
**Advantages:**

- Price discovery through active trading
- Institutional market makers provide liquidity (Susquehanna, Jane Street on Kalshi)
- Tighter spreads in liquid markets

**Disadvantages:**

- Liquidity fragmentation in less popular markets
- Requires market makers

#### **AMM (Polymarket International, Decentralized Platforms)**

**Mechanism:** Automated market maker using CPMM (Constant Product Market Maker) or LMSR (Logarithmic Market Scoring Rule)
**Advantages:**

- Always available liquidity
- No order book required
- Simplified UX

**Disadvantages:**

- Impermanent loss for liquidity providers
- Wide spreads in low-volume markets
- Susceptible to price manipulation in thin markets

**Paradigm Research (2024):** New pm-AMM design provides "more consistent liquidity and lower losses" but still experimental

### Fee Structures - CRITICAL COST ANALYSIS

| Platform | Trading Fee | Withdrawal Fee | Total Cost Example | Breakeven Hurdle |
|----------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Polymarket US** | 0.01% per contract | 0% | $10 on $100,000 | Negligible |
| **Polymarket Int'l** | 0% (spread only) | 2% net profits | $200 on $10,000 profit | 2.04% |
| **Kalshi** | 0.7-1.2% avg (probability-weighted) | Not disclosed | $1,200 on $100,000 | 1.2-1.5% |
| **PredictIt** | 10% gross profits | 5% withdrawal | $1,400 on $10,000 profit | **16%** |

**Fee Formula Comparison:**

**Kalshi Taker Fee:** `0.07 × contracts × price × (1-price)`

- At 50/50 odds: 1.75% per contract
- At 90/10 odds: 0.63% per contract
- Fees peak at even money, decline at extremes

**PredictIt Combined Drain:** On $10,000 position yielding $2,000 profit:

- Profit fee: $200 (10%)
- Withdrawal fee: $510 (5% of $10,200)
- Total: $710 on $2,000 gain = **35.5% of profits**

**Strategic Implications:**

- Use Polymarket for high-volume strategies (0.01% fee)
- Use Kalshi for extreme-odds positions (lower fees at tails)
- **Avoid PredictIt** except for unique markets with massive edge

### Settlement Processes

#### **Kalshi - Centralized, Pre-Defined Sources**

**Process:**

1. Market specifies official source upfront (e.g., "Federal Reserve official statement")
2. Outcome verified from designated source
3. Automatic settlement to user accounts
4. Disputes handled via customer service with regulatory oversight

**Advantages:**

- Fast, predictable settlement
- Minimal disputes
- Regulatory recourse

**Disadvantages:**

- Centralized trust required
- Source selection bias possible

#### **Polymarket - Decentralized UMA Oracle**

**Process:**

1. Market resolves based on UMA Protocol
2. Validators propose outcome
3. 2-hour challenge period
4. If challenged: UMA tokenholders vote
5. Economic incentives align toward truth

**Advantages:**

- Transparent, verifiable on-chain
- No single point of control
- Censorship-resistant

**Disadvantages:**

- Occasional disputed resolutions (Dec 2025 Polymarket US launch market)
- Slower settlement (2-hour minimum)
- Complexity for users

#### **PredictIt - Centralized Committee**

**Process:** Internal resolution committee with appeals process
**Assessment:** Similar to Kalshi but less regulatory oversight

### Dispute Resolution Risk

**Kalshi:** Lowest risk - CFTC oversight provides legal recourse
**PredictIt:** Low risk - DCM status provides regulatory framework
**Polymarket:** Moderate risk - decentralized but occasional controversial resolutions
**Augur/Gnosis:** High risk - no central authority, complex dispute mechanisms
